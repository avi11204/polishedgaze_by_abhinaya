Artist Profile

Meet Abhinaya

Owner, nail artist, and the person this website is built for.

Background & Motivation

  • Inspired by nail art from a young age through YouTube
  • Business idea confirmed at a cousin's wedding — saw nail art in real life and felt pushed to start
  • Self-made logo and brand colours — strong DIY identity
  • Competing in a crowded Coimbatore market; Shiny Claws is a nearby active competitor

Work Style & Character

  • Works from 4 am to 9 pm when needed — time is not a constraint
  • Each session takes 2–3 hours depending on design complexity
  • Deliberately avoids aggressive promotions — wants clients to return for the quality of her work
  • Self-aware about skill gaps: wants to improve structure, explore foreign techniques, and new products

No automated responses. Abhinaya prefers personal interaction — she reads requirements, asks questions, and provides custom inputs to clients before confirming. She wants it to be user-centric, not robotic.

04
Pain Points & Insights

Where things break down

Six friction points identified across the research.

₹?

No clear pricing

Most cited frustration (~52%). Users drop off when pricing is hidden or requires a DM to find out. Pricing transparency is the #1 conversion lever.

Critical
⏱

Slow response

No auto-responder on Instagram. Users lose interest or move to competitors before Abhinaya can respond manually — especially during busy sessions.

High
🖼

No design references

Users can't visualise options before booking — leading to mismatched expectations, revision requests, and rescheduling that eats into session time.

High
📋

Complicated booking

Multi-step DM → WhatsApp → call flow is confusing. No time slots means every booking is a manual coordination effort from both sides.

Medium
📢

No promotions / visibility

Competitors like Shiny Claws actively promote. Abhinaya relies purely on word-of-mouth — a healthy brand philosophy but limiting for new client discovery.

Medium
💬

No design guidance given

One user noted: the artist should proactively guide clients away from designs that won't work well for their nail shape, rather than waiting to be asked.

Low–medium

Feature Recommendations

What the website needs

Prioritised by user impact and business value.

Core — booking
Shop — vanity kits
Blog — education
Future scope
  • Must have

    Design gallery

    Categorised, filterable nail design portfolio. The #1 thing users want to see before booking — without it, every visitor is flying blind.

  • Must have

    Clear pricing page

    Tiered or per-design pricing, no hidden costs. Resolves the top pain point immediately and reduces DM volume significantly.

  • Must have

    Easy contact / booking CTA

    WhatsApp link, phone number, and optionally an online booking form. Meets users on the channels they already prefer.

  • Should have

    Reviews & testimonials

    57% cite reviews as a booking factor. Existing 5-star clients can build trust with new visitors organically.

  • Should have

    About / artist story

    Abhinaya's journey — from YouTube inspiration to cousin's wedding to Coimbatore studio — is a compelling narrative. Use it.

  • Must have

    Vanity kit shop page

    A dedicated section to browse and purchase kits curated by Abhinaya. Targets DIY beginners, gift buyers, and existing clients for at-home upkeep — revenue that works even when she's fully booked.

  • Should have

    Kit + service bundle

    E.g. "Book a session + take home a starter kit at ₹X off." Increases average order value and gives clients a reason to come back.

  • Should have

    Product detail with tutorial link

    Each kit links to a blog post or reel showing how to use it. Drives both trust and blog traffic simultaneously.

  • Nice to have

    "Gift a kit" CTA

    A dedicated gift flow: pick kit → personalise note → checkout. Directly targets the gift-buyer segment — including the curious non-nail-person from our survey.

  • Should have

    Blog / learn section

    Beginner-friendly nail care guides, design inspiration, product how-tos. Keeps users on the site longer, builds SEO, and positions Abhinaya as an expert.

  • Should have

    Blog linked to shop + booking

    Every post ends with a relevant kit recommendation or a "want this look? book a session" CTA. The blog becomes a conversion funnel, not just content for content's sake.

  • Nice to have

    Design inspiration guide

    Help clients pick designs based on occasion, nail shape, or vibe. Reduces back-and-forth DMs and helps clients arrive with realistic expectations.

  • Future scope

    "What nail vibe are you?" quiz

    Interactive quiz guiding users to a design style, then to a kit or booking CTA. High engagement, great for Instagram sharing.

  • Future scope

    Online appointment slot picker

    Calendar + WhatsApp redirect. Reduces manual coordination without needing a full booking system built from scratch.

  • Future scope

    Instagram auto DM responder

    Quick reply with pricing link and website URL for discovery traffic. Solves the slow-response pain point without removing Abhinaya's personal touch for serious enquiries.

Blog Strategy

Content that converts

Five blog post ideas, starting with the one that came straight from a survey outlier.

💅
From a real survey response

"Not a nail person? Here's why you might want to be"

One respondent said: "Why should I stay on a nails website unless I have a gf?" — so we turned it into a blog post. Targets curious visitors, gift buyers, and partners. Light, fun, low-barrier. Ends with a kit CTA. This is the post that brings in people who never intended to come.

For the uninitiated High conversion
📋
Beginner guide

"Your first nail appointment — what to expect, what to ask"

Reduces first-timer anxiety. Pre-answers the questions that flood her DMs. Ends with a booking CTA.

👰
Design guide

"How to pick the right nail design for your occasion"

Bridal, casual, office. Helps personas self-qualify and showcases Abhinaya's full design range.

🧴
Product how-to

"What's inside the Polished Gaze vanity kit — and how to use it"

Doubles as a product page. Builds DIY buyer confidence. Drives shop sales directly from content.

⚠️
Nail care 101

"Nail care mistakes most people make (and how to fix them)"

Educational, shareable, SEO-friendly. Positions Abhinaya as an expert, not just a technician.

Strategic Insight

The website's job is not to
hard-sell — it's to get out of
the way.

Abhinaya doesn't want to push promotions. She wants clients to return for the quality of her work. A well-designed website does exactly that — it shows the work, builds trust, and removes friction to book. The blog and shop extend her revenue beyond the chair, even when appointment slots are full. Together, they make Polished Gaze more than a studio — they make it a brand.
